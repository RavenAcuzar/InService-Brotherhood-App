ABOUT ISB

  • “We share the wisdom of leading by service and leading through service.”
    ~Dato Sri Vijay Eswaran

     

    Whether big or small in scale, every V event is provided with on-site support in carrying out its back-end requirements by a special group of individuals. They facilitate preparations needed before and after each event. They are comprised of Independent Representatives (IR) who were chosen to provide tireless service to their fellow IRs and are instrumental to the success of every V event. This is the InService Brotherhood.

     

    The InService Brotherhood had its humble beginnings in 1999, which saw the V starting with small events such as business presentations, in which the V Partners themselves acted as crew members.

     

    In a couple of years, V events grew in frequency and size. In 2001, the very first V-Convention was held in Bali, Indonesia, and for the first time in the V event history, a group of leaders was formed to provide service for the event. Even with a lack of a formal training, these selected individuals performed quite an exemplary task. After all, there is no one better to serve but members of The V family themselves who are keen on being of assistance to their fellow V brothers and sisters.

     

    Over the years, the V-Convention has established itself as the flagship event of The V—making its way across the globe to numerous countries such as Africa, Asia, Europe, and the Middle East. From mere hundreds, the attendees of the V-Convention grew to thousands, which has established the event as a grand spectacle anticipated by all members of the V community.
     

    With each V-Convention surpassing the last, the need for more InService members became more evident. In March 2007, the InService Brotherhood was officially formed during the very first InService Brotherhood Bootcamp held in Camp John Hay in Baguio City, Philippines.

     

    “We believe that the greatest leader is the greatest server.”
    ~Joseph ‘Japadas’ Bismark

     

    Independent Representatives were chosen to take part in the special training to prepare them in providing excellent and unmatchable service expected in every V-Convention. The V is the only company to have top leaders as volunteers to run events and provide quality service to the large-scale network. 

     

    Since 2007, the Core InService has guided the IR volunteers. V Partner Pathman Senathirajah also known as Chief took charge of the Core ISB, which members include esteemed networkers and corporate leaders. The Core InService exudes true servant leadership and selflessness. This group of people, along with the other members of the InService brotherhood, never failed to deliver successful events throughout the years.

     

    With the first InService Brotherhood Bootcamp widely received with excitement, it didn’t take long before it was molded and recognised as a yearly V tradition. It has become a must-attend event for all members who aspire to become servant leaders.

     

    Being accepted into the InService Brotherhood has become an honor and a privilege, as it provided the best avenue not only for personal growth and development, but also for fulfilling The V’s core DNA that is RYTHM (Raise Yourself To Help Mankind). In placing the welfare and success of others as priority, leaders are able to grow beyond their titles and lift others up through their selfless service and leadership.

     

    All members of the InService Brotherhood are called to follow a path of dedicated and impassioned service, of unwavering humility, and of exemplary leadership.

     

  • The InService Brotherhood Logo is composed of several symbolic elements that truly characterise the nature and goals of the InService Brotherhood.

     

    The V Logo - The superimposed V Logo is placed in the middle to signify the importance and central role of The V in the InService Brotherhood.  It denotes that everything that the ISB does is in service of and for the welfare of V network.

     

    The Wings - Taken from the admirable story behind the V formation the Canadian geese in flight during migration, the wings symbolise the InService Brotherhood’s goal to soar and empower those who are humble to learn and appreciate the knowledge of others. It also spans the limitless learning through righteous mentors who help spread their own wings.

     

    The Shield - The InService Brotherhood aims to strengthen and to support the ideals of The V through service. They also carry a protective mindset that allows them to safeguard the interests of the brotherhood at all costs.

     

    The Colours - The colours chosen for the InService Brotherhood logo manifest earth tones of green and brown. They are inherently pleasing to the eyes, and complement other colours very well. They symbolise the ISB’s dependability and dedication to fairness and equality— the undying belief that anyone can serve.

     

    With these elements together, they bring forth a message of empowerment and unity that are unique and unparalleled elsewhere.

     

  • All members of the InService Brotherhood are bound by an oath, a cause, and a mission greater than their own.

     

    The InService Brotherhood lives by two beliefs: Service Above Self and Paying It Forward. These mottos are ingrained in their every thought and action, guiding the InService comrades to perform their roles and functions to the best of their abilities, whenever or wherever they are needed.

     

     

    Service Above Self

     

    “V-Convention works because of InService.” ~Dato Sri Vijay Eswaran

     

    The concept of InService is unique to The V as an organization. It works from the noble idea that the best form of leadership is through service. The InService Brotherhood aims to make leaders better in recognizing that their power does not lie in their title but in their willingness to serve. We serve to lead.

     

    Dato Sri Vijay Eswaran asserts that “InService is a beautiful concept because we share the wisdom of leading through service.” For every leader who volunteers as InService, there is thus a clear acceptance, understanding, and commitment to placing service above self.

     

    One must not expect accolades and recognition while in service. It is a humbling experience, and not a stage for acknowledgment. To be in service is an honor and a privilege. We must raise ourselves higher so that we can serve better. The more prepared and open we are to service, the better we can uplift those whom we serve.  Service is our greatest asset, greatest weapon, and most of all, it is our purpose of who we are. In the words of Dato Sri Vijay, “If you are born to lead, you must be born to serve.”

     

    It is in service that we give. It is in service that we live. Let this be your vocation.

     

    Paying It Forward

     

    To be blessed with a life of comfort and financial freedom is not something that should be taken for granted. Most members of the InService Brotherhood have been vastly rewarded due to their hard work and perseverance in the network marketing business, partnered with the guiding principle of RYTHM, all are called to pay it forward.

     

    “You can never pay back; but you can always pay forward” – Woody Hayes

     

    Financial freedom is considered the ultimate reward in the network marketing business, and all Independent Representatives aspire for this. However, the teachings of the InService Brotherhood remind its members that one’s goal must not end with amassing worldly riches, that these material possessions must be utilised to create a better world for all of.

     

    “In the order of nature we cannot render benefits to those from whom we receive them, or only seldom. But the benefit we receive must be rendered again, line for line, deed for deed, cent for cent, to some- body.” ~Ralph Waldo Emerson

     

    Living by this motto will raise many questions in every individual, and at first, one’s reasons for paying it forward may seem selfish. The ego is a strong and powerful force, and one may at first feel compelled to pay it forward in order to gain more. Reciprocity is the first instinct of any individual, but with continuous practice of paying it forward, one will come to realize that the desire to pay it forward for one’s personal goals will diminish, and learn of the inherent satisfaction that comes with being able to help others.

     

    “You don’t pay love back; you pay it forward.” ~Lily Hardy Hammond

     

    Living a life of service gradually becomes part of one’s nature. Each person has to be of service to another – both as a responsibility and a privilege. Being able to help another is not something that comes easily at first, but at the very core of this gesture is man’s true purpose, to honorably be of service to the community.

     

  • The InService Brotherhood cheer has been an extremely powerful motivator since its first use in 2005 at a Networking Seminar Series event in Addis Ababa, Ethopia. Adapted from a traditional Ethiopian cultural shout, the ISB cheer is a simple way to declare the strength of the Brotherhood and the values that it stands for.

     

    The Cheer

    Tetena! Yen Ya New! Tene Kare! Yen Ya New! Taman Yinat! Yen Ya New!

     

    Translation

    Humility! It’s Ours! Strength! It’s Ours! Honesty! It’s Ours!
