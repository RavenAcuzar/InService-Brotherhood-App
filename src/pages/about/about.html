ABOUT ISB

  • The InService Brotherhood had its humble beginnings in the year 1999, where the V started with small business presentations, and the V Partners themselves were just crew members.

    Over the years, the V events grew in both frequency and size. The very first V-Convention (V-CON) was held in Bali and Indonesia in the year 2001, and for the first time in the history of the V, there was a group of Independent Representative (IR) leaders who came together to help run the event.

    From that time, the presence of the InService members became more crucial to the organization of V-events especially after the networks growth across the globe where the number of attendees had grown  to thousands and the events became bigger and more  spectacular with time.


    The InService Brotherhood was officially formed in March 2007 during the first ISB held in Camp John Hay in Baguio City, Philippines where IRs were chosen to take part in the special training to be servant leaders.

    The V is the only company to have top leaders as volunteers to run events and provide quality service to the large-scale network.

    Since the first ISB in 2007, the V Managing Director Pathman Senathirajah was assigned the role of Chief where he took charge of the Core ISB, whose members included esteemed networkers and corporate leaders.

    Since then, ISB has been molded and recognized as a yearly V tradition training event and became a mandatory event for all members who aspire to become servant leaders. More than that, being accepted into the InService Brotherhood has become an honor and a privilege; providing an avenue for learning and personal growth and development where all members are called to follow a path dedicated to impassioned service, unwavering humility, and exemplary leadership.

  • The ISB Logo is composed of several symbolic elements that characterize the nature and goals of this boot camp.

    The V Logo – It is placed in the middle to signify the importance of The V to the InService Brotherhood and shows that everything the ISB does is to serve the welfare of the V network.

    The Wings – The idea of the wings was taken from the nature of the eagle who taught its eaglets how to fly and the power of flight. And in this logo, it symbolizes the InService Brotherhood where the goal is teach networkers to learn to appreciate the knowledge of others by learning from righteous mentors to spread their own wings. In other words, the wings symbolize empowerees in others to soar by giving them the right mindset and attitude.

    The Shield – The purpose of the shield is to highlight the purpose of The InService Brotherhood that aims to strengthen and support the ideals of The V through service.

    The Colors - The colors chosen for the InService Brotherhood logo are earth tones (green and brown). As observed in mother nature itself; they symbolize ISB’s dedication to fairness and equality which is the undying belief that anyone can serve.  These two colors are inherently pleasing to the eye and they also complement other colors. They symbolize the ISB’s dedication to fairness and equality—

    Gathering all these elements together brings fourth a message: ‘Empowerment and Unity’ which is unique and unparalleled elsewhere.

    • Service Above Self.
    • Paying It Forward.

    These two mottos are ingrained in every thought and action, guiding the InService comrades to perform their roles to the best of their abilities whenever or wherever needed. These principles are instilled during the InService Brotherhood Bootcamp.

          Paying It Forward

    To be blessed with a life of comfort and financial freedom is not something that should be taken for granted. Most members of the InService Brotherhood have been vastly rewarded due to their hard work and perseverance in the network marketing business, coupled with the guiding principle of RHYTHM. All are called to pay it forward.

However, the InService Brotherhood reminds its members that one’s goal must not end with amassing wealth, but using these materials to create a better world for all of mankind.

  • ISB has four rankings, each with specific qualifications and merits as follows:

    Aspirant

    An Aspirant is a newcomer and a contender for the opportunity to serve. An Aspirant is a student of the InService Brotherhood and serves as a possible successor of a servant-leader based on the virtues of humility and service above self.

       QUALIFICATIONS

    • Must be an IR of QNET.
    • Has attended and participated in V-Conventions.
    • Has served at a ReVCon, NSS, and any local V Event.
    • mentioned above.
    • Has attended and participated in the ISB Bootcamp.

        RANKING: 1 Star.

    Apprentice

    An Apprentice is a trainee for InService Duty in all V Events.

         QUALIFICATIONS

    • Has attended and qualified in the InService Brotherhood Bootcamp.
    • Has served and assisted a Department Head (HOD) at V-CON, ReVCon, NSS and/or any local V Event.

          RANKING: Service Wings with 2 Stars.

    Accolade

    An Accolade is a determined Apprentice and a servant-leader recognized by the Core InService.

         QUALIFICATIONS

    • Has served as a Department Head in four (4) V-Cons and five (5) ReVCons.
    • Has served as InService in his respective country.
    • On call 24/7.
    • Has facilitated training in various V Events.

         RANKING: Service Wings with 3 Stars.

    Airborne

    An Airborne is the second highest rank in the InService Brotherhood Alliance and is a servant-leader and trainer or facilitator.

         QUALIFICATIONS

    • Has served as InService Chief, Assistant Crew Chief, or Department Head in six (6) V-Cons and ten (10) ReVCons.
    • Has facilitated and co-facilitated in V Events and other local events.
    • Has facilitated other events outside of their respective teams and in other countries.
    • Has served at least five (5) times as an ISB.

          RANKING: Service Wings with 4 Stars.

    Ace

    The Ace is the highest grade one can achieve in the InService Brotherhood.

           QUALIFICATIONS

    • Has facilitated large scale V events outside his respective team and country.
    • Has served as a facilitator in other team events held in various countries.
    • Has been invited to be a part of the Core InService.

            RANKING: Service Wings with 5 Stars.

    Uniting Different Teams - A Unique InService Brotherhood Trait

    The InService Brotherhood brings togother men and women

    The InService Brotherhood brings together men and women of all ages, who speak a multitude of languages, practice different religions, and hail from different countries. Members of the InService Brotherhood also part of different teams in their network marketing business.

    Although, these individuals, belong to different teams, find themselves side by side during V events, filled with the honor and the prestige to be able to serve collectively.

    Respect for one another is a value learned as a member of the InService Brotherhood. No matter what barriers exist, the Brotherhood is founded on the belief and  practice of service above one’s self.

  • The InService Brotherhood cheer has been an extremely powerful motivator since its first use in 2005 at a Networking Seminar Series event in Addis Ababa, Ethiopia. Adapted from a traditional Ethiopian cultural shout, the ISB cheer is a simple way to declare the strengths and the values that the Brotherhood stands for.

    The Cheer

    Tetena! Yen Ya New! Tene Kare! Yen Ya New! Taman Yinat! Yen Ya New!

    Translation

    Humility! It’s Ours! Strength! It’s Ours! Honesty! It’s Ours!
